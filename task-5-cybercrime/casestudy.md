## What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the deliberate and repeated use of digital technologies — including social media platforms, messaging applications, online gaming communities, and email — to harass, threaten, humiliate, or cause harm to another person. Unlike traditional bullying, it is not confined to a physical space or time; it follows victims into their private lives at any hour of the day or night.

---

## Case Study: The Group Chat Incident

**Setting:** A first-year engineering college in India, 2025.

**What Happened — Step by Step:**

1. A first-year student (let's call her Priya) shared a personal opinion about a course in her 60-member class WhatsApp group.
2. Two students began mocking her response publicly in the group with sarcastic replies and memes.
3. Others joined in, reacting with laughing emojis and forwarding her message with edited, humiliating captions to other groups.
4. Within hours, the content had spread to three other batch groups and an Instagram story — entirely without Priya's knowledge or consent.
5. Priya began receiving direct messages from people she did not know, continuing the harassment.
6. She left all college-related groups, stopped attending online sessions, and her academic performance declined significantly over the following weeks.

**Who is Usually Targeted?**
New students, those who express opinions that stand out, students from different cultural backgrounds, or anyone perceived as "different" by a dominant group.

**Consequences:**
- Severe anxiety, social withdrawal, and loss of confidence for the victim.
- Permanent digital record of the harassment — screenshots do not disappear.
- Legal consequences for perpetrators under **Section 67 of the IT Act, 2000** and **Section 354D IPC** (stalking/harassment).
- Disciplinary action from the institution.
