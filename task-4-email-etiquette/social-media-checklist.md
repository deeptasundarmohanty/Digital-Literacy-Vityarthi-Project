# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 4 | Deeptasundar Mohanty | 25BCE10884

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** — employers, internship coordinators, and faculty often search your name online before interviews or recommendations. |
| 2 | **Use privacy settings** — set personal accounts to private and review who can see your posts, stories, and tagged content. |
| 3 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" |
| 4 | **Give credit** — if you share someone else's work, idea, or photo, always acknowledge the original creator. |
| 5 | **Report harmful content** — if you see bullying, misinformation, or abusive content, use the platform's reporting tools rather than engaging or amplifying it. |
| 6 | **Use social media to build your portfolio** — share your projects, certifications, and achievements on LinkedIn and GitHub to create a positive digital footprint. |
| 7 | **Verify before sharing** — always check whether a piece of news is from a credible source before forwarding it, especially in group chats. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't post complaints about professors, classmates, or your institution publicly** — use official channels to raise concerns; public posts can have lasting professional consequences. |
| 2 | **Don't share personal information** such as your home address, phone number, daily schedule, or financial details on any public platform or group chat. |
| 3 | **Don't engage with or forward hostile content** — reacting to or sharing bullying content, even with a laughing emoji, makes you a participant. |
| 4 | **Don't use social media during classes or exams** — it is disrespectful to instructors and harmful to your own focus and academic performance. |
| 5 | **Don't impersonate others** — creating fake profiles or pretending to be someone else online is both unethical and illegal under Indian IT law. |
| 6 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** — especially on platforms where you share personal content or communicate privately. |
| 7 | **Don't post in anger** — if something upsets you online, wait at least 24 hours before responding publicly. What feels justified in the moment can be damaging in retrospect. |

---

> **Remember:** Your digital footprint is permanent. Content deleted from your account may still exist in screenshots, caches, or archives. Build an online presence you would be proud of four years from now.
